Funded project

EMERGENCE: Healthcare Robotics for Frailty in the Community

Funder: EPSRC Healthcare Technologies Network+

EMERGENCE was an EPSRC Healthcare Technologies Network+ project focused on building a sustainable ecosystem that connected researchers, industry, healthcare providers, service users, regulators and policy makers. Its aim was to support the development and adoption of robotic technologies that can help people living with frailty in community settings.

  • Supporting world-class healthcare robotics research and development for people living with frailty in UK communities.
  • Connecting researchers, industry and healthcare providers to strengthen the infrastructure needed for translation and adoption.
  • Using living lab test beds and funded feasibility studies to support co-designed, high-quality research.
  • Contributing guidance and resources through the EMERGENCE Body of Knowledge, including the White Paper on Robotics in Health and Social Care.

Funded project

Trailblazers Training Together

Funder: Ageing Well Service Development Fund / BNSSG Clinical Commissioning Group

A project exploring how telepresence robots and digital prompting can help volunteers support older people at home, encouraging social interaction and physical activity without requiring travel or complex technology setup by the client.

  • Uses telepresence robots to enable remote volunteer support in people’s homes.
  • Explores social connection, physical activity support and reduced digital exclusion.
  • Delivered with partners including Age UK Bristol, Fitness for Life, Bristol Health Partners, Robotics for Good CIC and University of Nottingham evaluation input.

Funded project

METRICS: Metrological Evaluation and Testing of Robots in International Competitions

Funder: European Union Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme · Grant agreement No. 871252

METRICS developed reproducible and objective approaches for evaluating robots through international benchmarking competitions. The project organised competitions across four priority areas: healthcare, agri-food, inspection and maintenance, and agile production.

Within METRICS, Praminda Caleb-Solly led the healthcare workstream, HEART-MET, which focused on benchmarking physically assistive robots in realistic healthcare and domestic support scenarios.

  • Led the healthcare workstream focused on assistive robotics evaluation and benchmarking.
  • Contributed to HEART-MET, a healthcare robotics challenge evaluating robot capabilities for socially acceptable item delivery and assistive interaction.
  • Supported development of objective, reproducible testing methods for robots operating outside controlled laboratory conditions.
  • Produced research on trustworthy robot benchmarking and the role of competitions in supporting evaluation, adoption and trust.
