Beyond Detection

Orchestrating Human-Robot-Robot Assistance via an Internet of Robotic Things Paradigm

Project Overview

Joseph Hunt, Koyo Fujii, Aly Magassouba, Praminda Caleb-Solly · ICSR 2025

Hospital patient falls remain a significant healthcare challenge. Many existing systems focus on detecting a fall or triggering an alert once risk is already high. This project instead investigates how robotic systems can support earlier, more proactive intervention.

The system combines privacy-preserving thermal sensing, real-time bed-exit prediction, and coordinated assistance from multiple robotic agents. A socially assistive robot interacts with the patient to understand their need, while a robotic arm can assist with practical support such as item delivery.

The project demonstrates how an Internet of Robotic Things approach can connect sensing, prediction, interaction, and physical assistance into a coordinated hospital-care scenario.

Key Achievements

  • Developed a modular Internet of Robotic Things framework.
  • Used low-resolution thermal sensing for privacy-preserving bed-exit prediction.
  • Connected sensing, prediction, patient interaction, and robotic assistance.
  • Demonstrated coordinated human–robot–robot interaction.
  • Moved beyond reactive alerts toward proactive patient support.
  • Explored how robots can respond to patient needs before risk escalates.
